Every AI prediction is a transaction. Without attribution, you're not part of it.

The protocol

Attribution measured live at inference, not reconstructed after.

AI responses are built from specific sources. Those sources rarely know which responses used their work, and they aren't paid for them.

The standard approaches don't fix this. Influence functions can estimate how much a training example shaped a prediction, but they're expensive and brittle. Machine unlearning is no better — "erased" data resurfaces and the model degrades in the process. Neither holds up in production. Syft takes a different path: the model queries a live network of sources at inference time, and contribution is recorded as it happens, against the sources that actually answered.*

The result is better predictions, grounded in live specialist knowledge, and a verifiable record of actual usage. That's what makes participation on your own terms possible.

Syft Protocol

The open standard for how AI systems find, query, attribute, and pay live data sources.

SyftHub

The registry. Where sources publish their endpoints and AI systems discover what to query.

SyftSpace

The secure data node. Creators run it to protect their IP. AI systems query it for live, attributable knowledge.

Who it's for

Rightsholders of every scale, and the AI teams that build with them.

AI development requires an abundance of trustworthy, high-quality data. Rightsholders require control, visibility, and fair terms for their IP. Syft is the inference-time infrastructure that unlocks this abundance, connecting both sides without compromise.

Individual creators

Participate through a collective, with no infrastructure to run yourself.

  • Set your own access policies
  • Get paid based on actual usage
  • Join a collective that negotiates leverage for you
  • Keep independence on terms

Media networks & enterprise publishers

Turn your archives into a high-margin, programmatic API.

  • Granular IP control: Define access policies down to specific publications, topics, or real-time feeds.
  • Audit-ready provenance: A cryptographic ledger of contribution for legal and regulatory compliance.
  • Hosted or self-hosted: Deploy SyftSpace nodes natively within your existing cloud (AWS/GCP).
  • Novel pricing models: Charge per request, document, or token, on your terms.

Ways to engage

Four levels of involvement.

We're in early access. Most people start at 01.

01
Track this. Stay informed as collectives form, pilots launch, and standards develop. No commitment beyond receiving updates.
02
Join a collective. Get matched with a collective licensing organization in your craft and jurisdiction. The collective runs the gateway, handles payments, and negotiates terms, while you set the policies that apply to your own work.
03
Pilot the infrastructure. Try per-query access on your own work, yourself or through a service that runs it for you. Pilots are small and reversible. Read the docs →
04
Coordinate publicly. Make the case, through your publications, channels, associations, or unions, that creators should keep control over how AI uses their work.
